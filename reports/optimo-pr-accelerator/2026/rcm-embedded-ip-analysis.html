6. Risks & Honest Tradeoffs

Risks of Embedding RCM

  • LLM cost multiplier: 5 perspective passes = 3–5× token cost. Mitigation: tiered RCM depth (Quick Scan vs. Full RCM); token-disciplined perspective prompts; cache shared context across perspectives.
  • Latency: 5 sequential perspective passes add latency. Mitigation: parallelize Explorer + Threat + Social (independent perspectives); only Evaluator depends on Explorer output.
  • Complexity: RCM output is rich but could overwhelm non-PR-native founders. Mitigation: progressive disclosure — show RCM Score + top insights first; full perspective breakdown on demand.
  • Over-engineering risk: Is 5-perspective reasoning overkill for a simple press release? Mitigation: RCM depth scales to task complexity. "Quick" = 2 perspectives (Explorer + Evaluator). "Full RCM" = all 5.
  • IP leakage: If RCM prompts are exposed client-side, competitors could study the methodology. Mitigation: RCM engine runs server-side only (Supabase Edge Functions); prompts are never client-visible.

Risks of NOT Embedding RCM

  • Commoditization: Without RCM, OptimoPR is "another AI PR tool" in a market where incumbents are adding AI features monthly. The window to differentiate on architecture closes fast.
  • Pricing ceiling: Feature-level AI tools compete on price ($25–$149/mo). RCM-powered reasoning commands premium pricing ($199–$499/mo). Not embedding RCM caps revenue per user.
  • Missed category creation: The opportunity to define "RCM-powered PR" as a category only exists once. If OptimoPR launches without RCM, a future competitor could invent their own multi-perspective reasoning and claim the category.
  • Weaker Optimo ecosystem leverage: RCM is Optimo-originated IP. Not using it in a PR venture that desperately needs differentiation is leaving Optimo's strongest asset unused.
